The **🔧 Configurator** tab is where a study stops being a row in a database and becomes something a participant can walk through. It defines the sequence of screens, splits the main task into parts, and attaches the questions you will later analyse.

Pick a study from the selector at the top of the panel first — without one you see *"Select a study from the list above to open the configurator."*

<Note>
The Configurator only serves studies in **builder mode**. Older studies show *"The configurator is available for studies in builder mode. This study uses the classic settings (Settings tab)."* — for those, everything lives in **⚙️ Settings**.
</Note>

## Its relationship to the Settings tab

The Configurator is not a separate editor. Both tabs render **one and the same builder form**; a function called `relocateKonfigSections()` then *physically moves* the configuration sections out of the Settings pane and into the Configurator pane. The sections tagged for relocation are:

| Section | What it does | Covered in |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 📄 Study screens | The participant's screen sequence | This page |
| 🔬 Experiment parts | Parts, layouts, timers, requirements, questions | This page |
| 🔀 Manipulation (A/B condition) | Between-subjects A/B split | [Manipulation](/guide/manipulation) |
| 📊 Metric conditions (social proof) | Randomised like/share counts | [Social proof](/guide/social-proof) |
| ⚙️ Conditional logic | "If → then" rules | [Conditional logic](/guide/conditional-logic) |

Everything else — **📋 Basic information** (study name, slug, description, title shown to participants), the study language selector and automatic translation, the visual post style, analytics and eye tracking, research-panel integration and the custom study domain — stays behind in **⚙️ Settings**.

<Tip>
The practical consequence: **Settings answers "what is this study?", the Configurator answers "what happens to the participant?"** Because it is one form, editing in either tab writes to the same record, and switching between the two tabs does not re-render or discard what you just typed.
</Tip>

## Study screens

Consent and debriefing carry an **Always** badge — they cannot be turned off, which is the platform enforcing the ethical floor of deception research rather than a UI limitation. The rest are optional.

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title="📋 Consent screen">
Free text shown before anything else. Leave it empty to fill in later — the study still runs with a default.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="🚫 Consent declined">
The message a participant sees after refusing to take part.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="📖 Instruction">
Toggleable. When on, you get the instruction text plus **Show a preview of the reaction icons (👍 / 👎 / 🔄 / 🚩)** — useful when you want participants to recognise the affordances before the feed starts, and worth turning **off** when noticing the reaction buttons is itself part of what you are measuring.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="👤 Demographics">
A single dropdown, **Position in the study flow**:

- **After consent (before the instruction) — default**
- **At the end (before the debriefing)**
- **Skip entirely**

*At the end* is often preferred when you worry that demographic questions could prime behaviour in the main task. *Skip entirely* suits recruitment from platforms that supply demographics out-of-band (Prolific, Lucid).

<Info>
This dropdown controls only **where** the demographics screen appears. The questions themselves live in the **👤 Demographics** tab. They are study content stored in the database and shown to participants, so they stay in the study's own language — they are not affected by the admin panel's PL/EN switch.
</Info>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="💬 Participant comments">
A master switch for the comment field under posts. With it on, participants can type under every post whose post editor has the comment field checked; with it off, the per-post setting stays inactive. Comments land in the export in the `post_N_comment` column.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="👍👎 Multiple reactions on one post">
By default reactions behave like radio buttons — a new one cancels the previous. Enabled, a participant can like, share and flag the same post simultaneously. **Like and dislike stay mutually exclusive.** A second click deselects. Every click is stored; undone reactions carry `is_undo=1` in the export, so the full interaction path stays visible.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="🎉 Debriefing">
Debriefing text, a **Show the list of posts marked true/false** toggle, and a **👁 Preview** button that opens the debriefing screen alone in a new tab — you can proofread it without walking the whole study.
</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Experiment parts

A study's main task is a list of **parts** (stored as `parts_json`). Each part has its own layout and its own rules, and you can chain several with transition screens between them — this is how you build within-subjects designs, or simply separate an exposure phase from a measurement phase.

Use **+ Add part** to append one, **✕ Remove** to delete it. The first part cannot be removed.

### Post layout

Each part carries its own **Post layout** toggle:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
<Card title="📜 Feed" icon="scroll">
Classic scrolling feed — all posts on one page. Higher ecological validity; participants control pace and can scroll back over what they saw.
</Card>
<Card title="📄 Pager" icon="file">
One post per page. Better control over exposure order and attention; makes per-post timing cleaner to interpret.
</Card>
</CardGroup>

### Transition screen

From the second part onwards you can enable **Show a transition screen before this part**, with its own text and a **Header emoji** (defaults to 📱; clear the field to hide the emoji entirely).

### Per-part behaviour

- **Show reaction buttons (👍 / 👎 / 🔄 / 🚩)** — turn off to hide interactions in this part.
- **Interaction with a post required** — any reaction on every post. Marked *legacy*; the structured requirements below take precedence over it.
- **Allow navigating back**.
- **⏱ Maximum time for this part** — seconds, `0` = no limit (accepted range 0–3600). When the time runs out the participant moves on to the next part automatically, **regardless of the state of the requirements**.

<Warning>
The timer overrides requirements. If you set both a hard time limit and a requirement, some participants will leave the part without satisfying it — decide in advance how you will treat those cases in analysis rather than discovering them in the data.
</Warning>

### 🎯 Interaction requirements

A structural list of *action × count* pairs a participant must satisfy before **Next** unlocks. Available actions:

- 👍 Likes
- 👎 Dislikes
- 👍👎 Like or Dislike (combined total)
- 🔄 Shares
- 🚩 Flags
- 💬 Comments
- ⭐ Any interaction — counts every reaction and comment together

Counts run 0–100. A count of **0** is not "impossible", it is **optional**: the participant sees an ∞ prompt and can do as many as they like without it blocking **Next**. An empty requirement list means no structural constraint at all.

<Warning>
Forcing interaction buys you complete data at the cost of spontaneity: a participant who must like three posts is no longer telling you what they *would* have liked. Requirements are a good fit when the interaction is the task; a poor fit when the interaction is the measure.
</Warning>

## Questions attached to parts

Questions belong to a **part**, not to the study as a whole, and each part decides how they reach the participant.

**❓ Question placement** offers:

- **after interaction (under the post)** — the default
- **after interaction (modal)**
- **together with the post**
- **after the post**
- **after all posts**

The first four measure reactions in the moment (with the post still visible or just seen); *after all posts* keeps the feed uninterrupted and measures afterwards, at the price of memory decay. You can also set a **Question screen title** and a **Subtitle / description**.

Each question added with **+ Add question** has a type and a **Required** checkbox:

<Tabs>
<Tab title="Open-ended">
Free text.
</Tab>
<Tab title="Single choice">
One option. Add options with **+ Option**.
</Tab>
<Tab title="Multiple choice">
Several options. Add options with **+ Option**.
</Tab>
<Tab title="Likert scale">
Configure **Scale points** (5–11), **Numbering from** (1 or 0), a **Min label**, a **Max label** and an optional **Description**.
</Tab>
</Tabs>

<Warning>
**Numbering from** changes what the stored numbers mean. The database records the number the participant actually clicked, so flipping a live question from 1 to 0 mid-collection reshuffles the apparent meaning of every answer already gathered. Set it before launch, or only on new questions.
</Warning>

## Saving and previewing

A **fixed action bar sits at the bottom of the Configurator**, always in view no matter how far you scroll:

<Steps>
<Step title="💾 Save">
Writes the whole builder — screens, parts and questions — immediately.
</Step>
<Step title="👁 Preview in a new tab">
Saves first, then opens the participant view at `/study/<slug>?preview=1` in a new tab. Preview sessions are marked as previews, so they do not pollute your data.
</Step>
</Steps>

Alongside the buttons sits a status line reading **Changes are saved automatically**, which flips to **Saved ✓** for a couple of seconds after each write.

### Autosave

You rarely need to press Save. Any edit in the Configurator schedules a silent save about **0.8 seconds** after you stop typing — short enough that your last change has landed before you reach the preview tab, long enough not to fire on every keystroke.

Some actions bypass the debounce and hit the server straight away: **+ Add question** creates the question immediately, and deleting one with **✕** removes it immediately — there is no confirmation step.

<Note>
The Preview button auto-creates a single example post if the study has no active posts, so that preview always works. If you see *"Added an example post for the preview"*, replace it with real content in the **✏️ Posts** tab.
</Note>

<Warning>
Conditional logic rules **do not fire in preview**. To check a screen-out or a part skip, you have to run the real flow.
</Warning>
