The Configurator tab is the central study builder for **builder-mode** studies. It is distinct from the Settings tab, which controls administrative metadata such as study name, language, and access codes. The Configurator is where you define what participants experience: the screens they see, the posts they read, and how the experiment is structured into parts. If you are working with a classic study, the Configurator tab is not available — classic studies are configured directly through their JSON definition.

<Note>
  The Configurator tab only appears for builder-mode studies. If you do not see it, check that your study was created in builder mode.
</Note>

## Study Screens

Every builder study follows a fixed screen order. Several screens are always present; others can be adjusted or skipped.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Consent Screen">
    The consent screen is always shown to participants before anything else. You can edit the consent text directly in the Configurator — use this field to provide your IRB-approved consent language, study duration estimate, and data usage information. Participants must actively confirm consent before proceeding. If a participant declines, they are routed to the **Consent Declined** screen.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Consent Declined Screen">
    This screen is shown automatically when a participant declines consent. You can customise the message to provide a polite exit and any relevant contact information. Participants who reach this screen do not proceed into the study and are not counted as active sessions.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Instruction Screen">
    The instruction screen is optional and can be toggled on or off in the Configurator. When enabled, it appears immediately after consent and before the first experiment part. Use it to explain the task — for example, asking participants to read posts carefully and react as they would on a real platform.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Demographics">
    The demographics block collects participant background information. You control its position in the study flow: immediately after consent, at the very end of the study, or skipped entirely. See the [Demographics guide](/guide/demographics) for details on defining questions and the methodological implications of placement.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Debrief Screen">
    The debrief screen is always shown at the end of the study, after all experiment parts and (if positioned there) demographics. Use it to explain the true purpose of the study, disclose any deception, and provide resources if your content covered sensitive topics. The debrief text is fully editable in the Configurator.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Study-Wide Behaviour Toggles

These switches apply to every post and every part of the study.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Participant Comments" icon="comment">
    When enabled, a comment textarea appears below each post, allowing participants to type a free-text response. Comments are saved per-post and exported alongside reaction data. Disable this if you want a clean reaction-only task.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Multiple Reactions on One Post" icon="face-smile">
    When enabled, participants can apply more than one reaction to a single post — for example, liking and flagging the same post. Like and dislike remain mutually exclusive regardless of this setting. When disabled, selecting any reaction replaces the previous one.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Experiment Parts

A study can contain one or more experiment parts. Each part is an independent block of posts shown to participants in sequence. Parts are useful for introducing between-block manipulations, separating topics, or creating multi-wave designs.

### Part Settings

Each part has the following configurable fields:

<ParamField path="name" type="string" required>
  A label for this part, shown only in the builder UI and exports. Not visible to participants.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="post_layout" type="Feed | Pager" required>
  Controls how posts are displayed. **Feed** renders all posts in a single scrollable list. **Pager** shows one post at a time and requires participants to advance manually.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="show_reaction_buttons" type="boolean" default="true">
  When enabled, like, dislike, share, and flag buttons appear on each post in this part. Disable this to create a read-only block where participants cannot react.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="interaction_requirements" type="structured rule">
  Optional minimum interaction requirements before a participant can advance past this part. Rules are structured (e.g. "at least 3 likes") and are validated at runtime. Participants see a progress indicator and cannot proceed until requirements are met.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="max_time" type="integer (seconds)">
  Maximum time in seconds a participant may spend on this part. Set to **0** to impose no time limit. When the timer expires, the participant is automatically advanced to the next part or screen.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="allow_navigating_back" type="boolean" default="false">
  When enabled, participants can return to previously seen posts within this part. Enabling this is appropriate for tasks that require comparison; disable it for studies measuring first-impression reactions.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="question_placement" type="string">
  Controls where per-post questions (if defined) appear relative to each post — for example, immediately below the post or on a separate page after each post in Pager mode.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="transition_screen" type="string">
  Optional text shown on a full-screen interstitial between this part and the next. Use transition screens to give participants a brief rest, introduce a new topic, or signal a change in task.
</ParamField>

### Adding Multiple Parts

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Configurator tab">
    Navigate to your study and select the **Configurator** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Scroll to Experiment Parts">
    The Experiment Parts section is below the study-wide toggles. Your study starts with one part by default.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a new part">
    Click **Add Part**. A new part card appears with default settings. Give it a descriptive name.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure each part independently">
    Expand each part card to set its layout, reaction settings, time limit, and other options. Parts are shown to participants in the order they appear in the list — drag to reorder.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign posts to parts">
    Once your study has two or more parts, each post in the Posts tab will display part-assignment checkboxes. A post can belong to one or more parts. See the [Posts guide](/guide/posts) for details.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Transition Screens

A transition screen is an optional interstitial displayed between two consecutive parts. You configure the transition text on the **preceding** part. The screen is shown after the last post in that part and before the first post in the next part. Leave the field blank to skip the transition entirely and move participants directly between parts.

## Optional Configurator Sections

The Configurator also houses three optional advanced sections. Each is collapsed by default and only activates when you enable it:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Manipulation (A/B)" icon="code-branch" href="/guide/manipulation">
    Define a binary between-subjects condition. Each post gains Headline A/B and Content A/B variant fields.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Metric Conditions" icon="chart-bar" href="/guide/social-proof">
    Control the engagement numbers (likes, shares, etc.) shown under posts as a second between-subjects factor.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Conditional Logic" icon="diagram-project" href="/guide/conditional-logic">
    Add deterministic if→then routing rules layered on top of random assignment.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
