---
title: "Posts and the post library"
description: "Build the stimuli participants see — source, headline, image, base metrics, A/B variants — and reuse them across studies via the post library."
---

Posts are your stimuli. Each one is a mock social-media item with a source, a headline, a body, optional image and avatar, and optional engagement counters. Everything a participant reacts to and rates comes from a post, so this is where most of your experimental design becomes concrete.

You edit posts in the **Posts** tab (the pane is titled **Post editor**). The tab works on one study at a time — select a study first, otherwise the editor tells you to.

## The post list

Every post is a collapsible row. Click the row header to open its form. The header shows, from left to right:

- the **order number** (`order_index`), which drives the sequence participants see;
- the **source name** and **handle**;
- a **Last edited** timestamp, once the post has been saved at least once;
- the **topic badge** (emoji + topic);
- a **TRUE** / **FALSE** badge — your ground-truth flag for the item;
- an **Active** / **Hidden** badge.

On the right of each row:

| Button | What it does |
| --- | --- |
| **👁 Preview** | Opens a preview of the post as participants would see it |
| **🗂 To library** | Copies the post into the shared post library (the study post stays where it is) |
| **↑** / **↓** | **Move up** / **Move down** — swaps the post's `order_index` with its neighbour |
| **🗑** | **Delete post** — asks for confirmation |

<Warning>
Deleting a post also deletes the reactions and ratings attached to it, and cannot be undone. The confirmation dialog says so explicitly. If you only want a post out of the feed, switch off **Active** instead — hidden posts keep their data.
</Warning>

Above the list there are two buttons: **+ Add post** creates an empty post at the end of the list and expands it; **🗂 From library** opens the library picker.

<Note>
A brand-new post is not blank in the database: it starts with topic `nauka`, emoji `📝` and **Time ago** `teraz`. Overwrite those with your own content.
</Note>

## Two editor layouts

The fields you see depend on how the study was created.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Studies built in the Configurator">
    Every study you create with **+ New** in the header opens in the Configurator and is flagged as a builder study (the study list marks it with a **Builder** badge). Its post form is the one described below: variant sections are labelled with your own condition names, base metrics are edited directly on the post, and comments under the post are a free list.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Older (legacy) studies">
    Studies created before the Configurator keep the older post form. It differs in four places, described in [Legacy post form](#legacy-post-form): fixed "Version A — manipulative / Version B — neutral" headings, a **Manipulation techniques** checklist, an **Experimenter comment** per variant, and **Metrics per condition** instead of plain base metrics.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Basics

| Field | Notes |
| --- | --- |
| **Topic** | A dropdown with five fixed values: `zdrowie`, `klimat`, `polityka`, `ekonomia`, `nauka`. This is study content, so it stays in the study's language and is translated per study, not by the panel's language switch. |
| **Emoji** | Free text, shown next to the topic on the post's badge. |
| **Source (name)** | The account name shown on the post, e.g. a fictional outlet. Also used to build the fallback avatar initials. |
| **Handle** | The `@`-style handle under/next to the source name. |
| **Time ago** | Free text, not a real timestamp — you type exactly what participants read (e.g. `2 h`). |
| **True post** | Toggle. Your ground truth for accuracy analyses; drives the TRUE/FALSE badge in the admin list. Participants never see it in the feed. |
| **Active** | Toggle. Only active posts are served to participants. |
| **Hide topic** | Toggle. Suppresses this post's topic badge for participants — useful when the category itself would cue credibility. |

### Experiment parts

If the study has two or more parts, the Basics grid also shows **Experiment parts** with a checkbox per part, prefixed by its position in the flow (`1.`, `2.`, …). Tick every part the post should appear in — one post can appear in several parts, which is how you show the same stimuli twice (e.g. a feed for reactions in part 1, then paged with questions in part 2). The hint under the checkboxes states the fallback: **Nothing ticked = the post goes to the first part.**

## Content and A/B variants

Each post stores two content variants in the database: `headline_a` / `content_a` and `headline_b` / `content_b`. This is what the **Manipulation** factor in the Configurator switches between — a participant assigned to the first condition sees the `_a` fields, one assigned to the second sees the `_b` fields.

What the form shows follows directly from that:

<Steps>
  <Step title="No manipulation configured">
    You get a single **Headline** and **Content** pair plus one **Image** upload. No A/B anywhere — the `_b` fields are not editable and stay empty.
  </Step>
  <Step title="A manipulation with two named conditions">
    You get two sections headed `Condition A: your label` and `Condition B: your label`, each with its own **Headline**, **Content**, and an **Image** area labelled with that condition. The first condition writes to the `_a` columns, the second to the `_b` columns.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
The manipulation is deliberately a **two-arm** split, because a post only carries `_a` and `_b` content. If a study were saved with three or more conditions, the participant runtime assigns only the first two — a third arm would be recorded but could not show distinct content.
</Warning>

<Tip>
Fill in the B variant for every post you intend to manipulate. In builder studies, an empty `headline_b` / `content_b` silently falls back to the A text, so a half-finished B condition looks like it works while actually showing condition A's stimulus to both groups.
</Tip>

## Social interactions (base metrics)

Under **Social interactions** — hinted with **(0 = hide)** — you set the engagement numbers printed on the post:

- **👍 Likes** → `base_likes`
- **🔄 Shares** → `base_shares`
- **👎 Dislikes** → `base_dislikes`
- **🚩 Flags** → `base_flags`
- **💬 Comments** — read-only; it **Updates automatically from the comment list below**.

How these interact with the study's metric conditions (the social-proof factor set in the Configurator) matters methodologically:

1. A base value greater than 0 on the post **always wins**.
2. Otherwise, if the assigned metric condition has a range (`max > 0`), the number is drawn at random from that range.
3. If neither applies, the counter is 0.

So pin a value on a post only when you want that specific post to escape the randomised social-proof manipulation — pinning values on every post disables the factor in practice.

<Note>
Whether counters are shown to participants at all is a study-level switch (**Show metric counters to participants**) in the Configurator, not a post setting.
</Note>

## Comments under the post

**💬 Comments under the post** is a list you build with **+ Add comment**. Each row has:

- **Author (nickname)**
- **Comment text**
- a likes number (**Comment likes**)
- an **✕** button to remove the row

These are fake comments displayed as part of the stimulus — a cheap and well-controlled way to manipulate perceived consensus. Rows with empty text are dropped on save, and the read-only **💬 Comments** counter tracks the list length.

<Note>
This is different from the participant's own comment field, which is the **💬 Comment field** toggle described below.
</Note>

## Avatar

Click the circle to upload an avatar (**jpg/png/webp, max 5 MB**). Without one, the post shows initials derived from the **Source (name)**. **Delete avatar** removes it and falls back to initials — the confirmation says exactly that.

Below it: **Show the avatar on this post (turn off for an anonymous source)**. Use it when a source should read as faceless while the rest of the feed keeps avatars.

## Visible interactions under the post

**Visible interactions under the post** — **(per-post; also limited by the study and part settings)** — is a row of toggles controlling which affordances this post offers:

**👍 Like**, **👎 Dislike**, **🔄 Share**, **🚩 Flag**, **💬 Comment field**.

All default to on. The hint is the important part: these are per-post *limits*, applied on top of the study- and part-level settings — a post cannot show an interaction the study has switched off.

The comment field is the one exception, and it's handled for you: if you tick **💬 Comment field** on a post while comments are off study-wide, saving the post also enables comments at the study level and tells you so (*"Comments were also enabled at the study level (required by the per-post toggle)."*). Other posts keep whatever their own toggle says.

## Images

Images upload immediately on selection — you do not need to press **Save post** for them. Accepted types are jpg/png/webp, up to 5 MB. Each variant has its own **Click to choose an image** area and a **Delete image** button that appears once an image exists.

<Info>
Uploaded images are stored in the database (as a blob) and served from `/uploads/<study_id>/<filename>`. Every upload gets a fresh filename, so replacing an image never leaves participants looking at a cached old one.
</Info>

## Saving

**Save post** writes the form; a **✓ Saved** marker appears briefly and the TRUE/FALSE and Active/Hidden badges in the row header update. Text fields are only persisted on save — images and avatars, as noted, are not.

## Order and what participants actually see

Ordering is controlled by the **↑** / **↓** arrows, which swap `order_index` between neighbouring posts. The participant feed is served ordered by part, then `order_index`, then id — so your reordering is reflected in what participants see.

Two study-level settings sit on top of this:

- Only posts with **Active** on are served.
- **Posts per session** (1–20) limits how many posts a participant gets.

## The post library

The library is a study-agnostic catalogue of reusable posts. Its purpose is to stop you rebuilding the same stimuli by hand for every study — and, more usefully, to let a lab converge on a stable, reusable stimulus set across experiments.

<Warning>
The post library is **global**: it is shared by every account on the installation, unlike studies, which are private to their owner. Anything you promote to the library is visible to — and deletable by — every other researcher on the same instance.
</Warning>

### Promoting a post to the library

Click **🗂 To library** on any post row (*"Save this post to the library for reuse"*). There is no dialog: the entry is created immediately and you get a *"Post saved to the library."* confirmation. The study post itself is untouched — this creates a copy in the catalogue.

The name and category are derived automatically:

- **Name**: `source · topic` when both exist, otherwise the source, otherwise the topic, otherwise a trimmed headline.
- **Category**: the post's topic — which is what the picker's category filter uses.

Everything content-related is copied, including both content variants, both images, the avatar, base metrics, comments, and the per-post visibility toggles. What is *not* copied is anything study-specific: the study id, the order index, and the part assignment.

### Adding a post from the library

**🗂 From library** in the Posts toolbar (*"Copy a ready-made post from the reusable library"*) opens the **🗂 Post library** modal. Each template is a card with its thumbnail, a TRUE/FALSE badge, the name, source, and headline.

If any templates carry categories, a filter bar appears above the grid with an **All** button and one button per category.

Click **➕ Add to study** to copy the template into the currently selected study; it lands at the end of the post list and the modal closes.

### Copy-on-add

This is the rule worth internalising, and the modal states it outright: *"Click "➕ Add to study" to copy the template into the current study. The copy is independent — later changes to the template do not affect it."*

<Check>
The copy is a true copy, images included. After adding:
- editing the study's post never touches the library template;
- editing the library template never touches posts already copied into studies;
- deleting the library template leaves every copied post intact.
</Check>

This is a deliberate design choice, not a limitation. A live link would mean a template edit could silently change the stimuli of a study that is already collecting data — which would quietly invalidate it. Copy-on-add makes each study's stimulus set frozen and self-contained at the moment you build it.

The trade-off: fixing a typo in a template does **not** propagate. Studies already using it keep the typo, and you fix each one by hand.

### Deleting from the library

The **🗑** button on a card (**Delete from library**) removes the catalogue entry. The confirmation is explicit: *Delete "…" from the library? Posts already copied into studies remain untouched.*

When the library is empty, the modal says: *"The library is empty. Use "🗂 To library" on any post to add the first template."*

## Legacy post form

Studies created before the Configurator use an older post form. Basics, avatar, visible-interaction toggles, images, and ordering work the same; four things differ.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Fixed variant headings">
    Instead of your own condition labels, the two content sections are headed **Version A — manipulative** and **Version B — neutral**, with **Headline A** / **Content A** and **Headline B** / **Content B**. They write to the same `_a` / `_b` columns. Images are labelled simply **Version A** and **Version B** under **Images (optional, max 5 MB — jpg/png/webp)**.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Manipulation techniques">
    A checkbox grid for tagging which techniques a post uses. The list is fixed and in Polish: `pilność`, `fałszywy ekspert`, `spisek`, `liczby bez źródła`, `emocjonalne słowa`, `kozioł ofiarny`. It is metadata for your own analysis — participants never see it.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Experimenter comment">
    One author + text pair per style condition, hinted **(shown only in conditions with 💬 enabled)**. The section labels come from the study's own condition labels. This is the legacy equivalent of the builder's comment list — a single planted comment rather than a free list.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Metrics per condition">
    Rather than plain base metrics, the legacy form shows a **👍 Likes / 🔄 Shares / 👎 Dislikes / 🚩 Flags** row **per active metric condition**, with the condition's range shown next to its label. The hint reads **(empty = random from the condition's range)** — leave a field blank to let the social-proof manipulation generate the number, fill it to pin that condition's value for that post.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
