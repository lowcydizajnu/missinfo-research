The Posts tab is where you create and manage every piece of content participants will see during your study. Each post represents a single social-media item — a news article headline, a tweet-style post, or any other content unit your study requires. Posts are study-specific: they are not shared across studies unless you copy them from the post library. You can add as many posts as your study needs, reorder them at any time, and control exactly which experiment parts each post appears in.

## Adding a Post

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Posts tab">
    Navigate to your study and click the **Posts** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Add Post">
    A new blank post card appears at the bottom of the list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the post fields">
    Complete the fields described below. At minimum, provide a Source name and either a Headline or Content.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save** on the post card. Unsaved posts are highlighted; navigating away without saving will lose your changes.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Post Fields

<ParamField path="source_name" type="string" required>
  The display name of the account or publication that "posted" this content. Shown as the author name in the post card — for example, *The Daily Wire* or *@sciencenews*.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="source_handle" type="string">
  The @handle shown below the source name. Include the @ symbol in the value if you want it displayed. Leave blank to show no handle.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="time_ago" type="string">
  A human-readable timestamp shown on the post, e.g. *2h*, *3 days ago*, or *Jan 14*. This is free text — it is not computed from a real date.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="topic" type="string">
  A category badge displayed on the post card, such as *Health*, *Politics*, or *Climate*. Use topics to group posts thematically. The topic label is visible to participants unless you enable Hide topic.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="emoji" type="string">
  A single emoji shown alongside the topic badge. Decorative only.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="hide_topic" type="boolean" default="false">
  When enabled, the topic badge and emoji are hidden from participants. The topic field is still populated and available in exports for your own categorisation.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="headline" type="string">
  The main headline of the post. Rendered in larger, bolder text. For A/B studies, this becomes **Headline A** — see [A/B Variants](#ab-variants) below.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="content" type="string">
  The body text of the post, shown beneath the headline. For A/B studies, this becomes **Content A**.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="is_true" type="boolean" default="false">
  Marks this post as factually accurate. Used in belief-accuracy analysis to identify which posts represent ground truth. In the export, this flag lets you compare participant credibility ratings against the known accuracy status of each post. Posts with `is_true` enabled are shown to participants like any other active post — this flag is for your analytical use, not a visibility control.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="is_active" type="boolean" default="true">
  When disabled, the post is excluded from all parts of the study. Use this to draft posts without participants seeing them, or to retire posts mid-study without deleting them.
</ParamField>

## Engagement Counters

Each post has four social-interaction counters. These set the **base values** displayed under the post:

| Counter | Field | Behaviour when set to 0 |
|---------|-------|------------------------|
| Likes | `base_likes` | Counter hidden entirely |
| Shares | `base_shares` | Counter hidden entirely |
| Dislikes | `base_dislikes` | Counter hidden entirely |
| Flags | `base_flags` | Counter hidden entirely |

Setting a counter to **0** hides that metric from participants — no zero is shown. Set it to any positive integer to display that number. If you have defined [metric conditions (social proof)](/guide/social-proof), a non-zero base value on an individual post **overrides** the condition's random range for that counter on that post.

## Image Upload

You can attach one image to each post. Supported formats are **JPEG, PNG, and WebP**. The maximum file size is **5 MB**.

<Note>
  Images are stored per-post and per-study. Uploading the same image to multiple posts creates separate copies. If you update an image, re-upload it on every post where it should change.
</Note>

To upload an image, click the image area on the post card and select a file, or drag and drop a file onto the image zone. To remove an image, click the remove icon that appears on hover.

## A/B Variants

When you have enabled a [Manipulation (A/B)](/guide/manipulation) in the Configurator, each post card gains four additional fields:

<ParamField path="headline_a" type="string">
  The headline shown to participants assigned to condition A. This replaces the standard Headline field for condition-A sessions.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="content_a" type="string">
  The body text shown to condition-A participants.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="headline_b" type="string">
  The headline shown to participants assigned to condition B.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="content_b" type="string">
  The body text shown to condition-B participants.
</ParamField>

You may also upload a separate image per variant. If no variant-specific image is provided, both conditions see the same image (or no image, if none is uploaded).

<Tip>
  For control posts — posts that should be identical across conditions — fill in the same text for both A and B variants, or leave B blank and ensure your manipulation design accounts for this. See the [Manipulation guide](/guide/manipulation) for the recommended approach.
</Tip>

## Assigning Posts to Experiment Parts

When your study has **two or more experiment parts**, each post card displays a set of checkboxes listing every part. Check the parts you want this post to appear in. A post can be assigned to multiple parts — it will appear independently in each one, and reactions recorded in each part are stored separately.

If your study has only one part, the assignment checkboxes do not appear and all active posts are automatically included.

## Reordering Posts

Use the **↑** and **↓** arrow controls on each post card to move it up or down in the list. The order in the Posts tab determines the default display order within a part using the **Feed** layout. In **Pager** layout, posts are presented in this same order, one at a time.

## Copying from the Post Library

The **From library** button opens a modal listing posts in your shared post library. Selecting a library post copies its fields — source name, handle, headline, content, counters, and image — into a new post in the current study. The copy is fully independent: editing it does not affect the library original.

<Tip>
  Use the post library to maintain a curated bank of validated stimuli that you can reuse across studies without re-entering data.
</Tip>

## Builder Comments

Each post supports a **builder comment thread** — a fake comment section visible beneath the post in the participant view. Builder comments are authored by you in the builder and are purely static: participants cannot reply to them or interact with them. Use them to simulate the social context of a real comment section, which can influence how participants perceive and respond to a post.

To add a builder comment, expand the **Comments** section on a post card and click **Add Comment**. Each comment has an author name, handle, and text body. Comments appear in the order you define them.
