Misinfo Research uses an invite-only account model — only administrators can create new accounts, and there is no public registration page. Every user belongs to one of two roles that determine which studies they can see and manage across the platform.

<Note>
  Self-registration is intentionally disabled. Participants never create accounts; only researchers and administrators who receive a direct invitation from an admin log in to the admin panel at `/admin`.
</Note>

## Roles

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Admin" icon="shield-halved">
    Sees and manages **every study** on the instance. Can create, edit, deactivate, and delete user accounts. Can also perform all researcher actions.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Researcher" icon="flask">
    Sees and manages **only their own studies** (and any studies shared with them). Cannot access account management.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Creating an Account

Only admins can add new users. Open the **Konta** tab in the admin navigation, then click **Dodaj konto** (Add account).

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enter credentials">
    Provide a **username** (used to log in) and an **optional email address**. Set a temporary **password** — it must be at least 8 characters long.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a role">
    Select either **admin** or **researcher**. You can change this later from the account edit view.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Zapisz** (Save). The new user can now log in at `/admin` with the credentials you provided and should change their password on first use.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Share credentials securely — for example via an end-to-end encrypted message. The platform stores all passwords as **bcrypt hashes** and never exposes them in plaintext after creation.
</Tip>

## Editing an Account

Click the edit icon next to any account in the **Konta** list to update:

- **Email address** — used for notifications and account recovery workflows.
- **Role** — promote a researcher to admin or demote an admin to researcher (subject to the lockout guard below).
- **Active / Inactive status** — inactive accounts cannot log in.
- **Password** — enter a new password of at least 8 characters to override the existing one.

Save your changes with **Zapisz**.

## Deactivating vs. Deleting an Account

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Deactivating an account">
    Setting an account to **Inactive** immediately invalidates the user's session and prevents future logins. All of their studies remain intact and attributed to them. You can reactivate the account at any time by switching the status back to **Active**.

    Use deactivation when a researcher is temporarily off the project or when you need to pause access without losing their work history.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Deleting an account">
    Deleting an account is **permanent and cannot be undone**. Before the record is removed, all studies owned by that user are automatically **reassigned to the admin who is performing the deletion**. The studies themselves — data, results, and configuration — are preserved.

    Use deletion only when you are certain the account is no longer needed and you have confirmed ownership of the reassigned studies.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Warning>
  Deleting a user immediately transfers ownership of all their studies to your account. Review the study list for that user before proceeding so you can redistribute ownership afterwards if needed.
</Warning>

## Lockout Guard

To prevent being locked out of the platform, the system enforces one hard rule: **the last active admin account can never be deactivated or demoted to researcher.** If you attempt either action, the platform will block the change and display an error.

To work around this, first promote at least one other user to admin, then modify the original account.

## Per-Study Collaboration

A researcher who owns a study can invite another researcher to co-edit that specific study without giving them broader access to any other study or to account management.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the study">
    Navigate to the study you want to share and open it in the builder or Configurator.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Accounts in the header">
    The **Accounts** button in the study header opens the collaboration panel, listing all researcher accounts on the instance.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Grant edit access">
    Toggle on the collaborator(s) you want to invite. They receive **full edit access** to this study only — they cannot see your other studies or manage accounts.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Revoke access">
    Return to the same panel at any time and toggle off a collaborator to remove their access immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Admins can view the collaborator list for every study on the instance. Researchers can only see and manage collaborators on studies they own.

## Login and Session Security

| Setting | Value |
|---|---|
| Login URL | `/admin` |
| Session token | JWT, valid for **24 hours** |
| Brute-force protection | Rate-limited to **20 attempts per 15 minutes** per IP |
| Password storage | bcrypt hashes |

After 20 failed login attempts within a 15-minute window, further attempts from that IP address are temporarily blocked. Wait for the window to expire or contact your server administrator to reset the limit.
