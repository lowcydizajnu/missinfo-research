Demographic questions capture background information about your participants — age, location, education level, and other characteristics relevant to your analysis. In Misinfo Research, demographics are **study content**: the questions and answer options you define are stored as part of the study itself, in the study's own language, and are entirely independent of any language setting in the admin panel. This means you can run two studies in different languages with different demographic instruments without any conflict.

<Note>
  Demographic question labels and answer options are not UI translations. They are content you write directly into the study. Changing the admin panel language has no effect on demographics.
</Note>

## Default Questions

Every new builder study is seeded with four default demographic questions. You can edit, reorder, or delete them. Their default export column names (derived from their **field keys**) are:

| Question | Default Field Key | Default Label | Type |
|----------|------------------|---------------|------|
| Age | `age` | Age | Radio |
| Residence | `residence` | Country of residence | Radio |
| Education | `education` | Highest level of education | Radio |
| Gender | `gender` | Gender | Radio |

The field key is the column header in your data export. If you change a field key after collecting data, the old and new exports will have different column names — rename keys only before launching your study.

## Adding a Custom Question

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Demographics tab">
    Navigate to your study and click the **Demographics** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Add Question">
    A new question card appears at the bottom of the list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter a field key">
    Choose a short, lowercase, underscore-separated identifier such as `political_affiliation` or `media_literacy_score`. This becomes the export column name.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write a label">
    Enter the question text participants will read, in your study's language. For example: *Which political party do you most closely identify with?*
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a question type">
    Choose from the four supported types described below.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add answer options (if applicable)">
    For Radio and Multiselect types, define each option. For Text and Number types, no options are needed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save** to commit the question to the study.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Question Types

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Radio">
    Presents a list of mutually exclusive options. Participants select exactly one. Suitable for questions like gender, education level, or any categorical variable where only one answer applies.

    Each option requires a **value** (stored in the export, e.g. `bachelors`) and a **label** (shown to participants, e.g. *Bachelor's degree*). Keep values short and machine-readable; labels can be as descriptive as needed.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Multiselect">
    Presents a list of options where participants may select any number. Suitable for questions like *Which social media platforms do you use?* The export stores selected values as a delimited list within the column.

    Define options the same way as Radio: each has a value and a label.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Text">
    A free-text input field. Suitable for open-ended questions such as *What is your occupation?* No options are defined. The participant's raw text is stored in the export column.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Number">
    A numeric input field. Suitable for age, years of experience, or any integer or decimal value. No options are defined. Validation ensures participants enter a valid number.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Defining Answer Options

For **Radio** and **Multiselect** questions, you define a list of answer options. Each option has two properties:

<ParamField path="value" type="string" required>
  The internal identifier stored in the export. Use lowercase, no spaces — for example, `strongly_agree` or `female`. Values must be unique within a question.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="label" type="string" required>
  The text shown to participants. Can include spaces, punctuation, and any characters your study language requires — for example, *Strongly agree* or *Femme / Woman*.
</ParamField>

To reorder options, use the ↑ and ↓ controls on each option row. The display order in the UI matches the order participants see.

## Reordering Questions

Use the ↑ and ↓ controls on each question card to change the order in which questions are presented to participants. Reordering questions does not affect their field keys or export columns.

## Demographics Position in the Study Flow

You control where the demographics block appears relative to the experiment content. Set this in the **Configurator** tab under the Study Screens section.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="After Consent" icon="arrow-right">
    Demographics are collected immediately after the participant gives consent, before seeing any posts. Ensures complete demographic coverage but may prime participants before they encounter stimuli.
  </Card>
  <Card title="At End" icon="flag-checkered">
    Demographics are collected after all experiment parts, just before the debrief screen. Minimises priming effects on post exposure and reactions.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Skip" icon="forward">
    No demographic questions are shown. Use this if you are collecting demographics through an external recruitment platform (e.g. Prolific) or if demographics are not relevant to your analysis.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Tip>
  Consider placing demographics **at the end** of the study when your stimuli touch on politically or socially sensitive topics. Asking about political affiliation or education before participants read posts can activate identity-relevant cognition and systematically shift their responses — a well-documented priming effect. Post-task placement preserves the ecological validity of your first-impression data.
</Tip>

## Field Keys and Exports

Field keys are the single most important naming decision you make for demographics. The key you assign becomes the column header in every data export for this study. Consistent, descriptive keys make downstream analysis far easier.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Key naming conventions">
    Use lowercase letters, digits, and underscores only. Avoid spaces, hyphens, or special characters. Examples of good keys: `age`, `country_of_birth`, `years_education`, `media_trust_score`.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Changing keys after data collection">
    Avoid renaming field keys once real participant data has been collected. The export will use the current key for all future sessions, creating a mismatch with earlier exports. If you must rename, reconcile column names in your analysis script.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Default question keys">
    The four seeded questions use the keys `age`, `residence`, `education`, and `gender`. You can rename these before launching, but take care: any external codebooks or pre-registration documents that reference these column names will need to be updated accordingly.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
