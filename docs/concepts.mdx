Every experiment you run in Misinfo Research is built from a small set of interlocking objects: a **Study** that defines the experiment as a whole, one or more **Parts** that structure the participant experience, **Posts** that make up the simulated social-media feed, **Conditions** that control what each participant sees, and **Sessions** that record what each participant did. Understanding how these objects relate to one another will help you design cleaner studies, avoid common data-quality pitfalls, and make sense of your exports.

---

## Study

A **Study** is the top-level container for an experiment. Everything else — parts, posts, conditions, sessions — belongs to exactly one study.

Each study has two separate name fields that serve different audiences:

- **Internal name** — visible only inside the admin panel, used to identify the study in lists, exports, and the browser tab. Choose something descriptive for your team.
- **Participant title** — displayed to participants at the top of every page they see. Keep it neutral to avoid demand characteristics.

### Slug and participant URL

Every study has a **slug** — a short, URL-safe identifier you choose when you create the study. The slug determines the participant link:

```
https://your-host/study/<slug>
```

The slug must be unique across your installation. Once you begin distributing the link, treat the slug as permanent; changing it breaks all previously shared URLs.

### Active vs inactive

A study can be **Active** (accepting real participant sessions) or **Inactive** (accessible only via preview). You activate and deactivate a study from the Settings tab at any time. Deactivating pauses data collection without deleting any data.

### Builder mode vs classic mode

Misinfo Research supports two study creation modes. All studies created via the **+ New** button use Builder mode. Older studies created before Builder mode was introduced are *classic* studies. The distinction affects how posts are assigned to conditions and how randomization works.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Builder">
    Builder-mode studies use the **Configurator** tab as the central editing interface. You define consent text, instruction screens, demographics placement, and one or more experiment parts through a structured form. Conditional logic rules, post questions, and multi-part sequences are all managed in the Configurator.

    Condition assignment in Builder studies is **uniformly random**: each incoming session is independently and randomly assigned to one of the available conditions with equal probability.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Classic">
    Classic studies were created before the Configurator existed. Their structure is defined through a combination of study-level settings and post-level flags rather than through a unified interface. Some features available in Builder mode — such as conditional logic rules and post questions — are not available in classic studies.

    Classic studies use **permuted block randomization**: participants are assigned to conditions in balanced blocks, ensuring that condition counts stay approximately equal throughout recruitment rather than converging only in expectation.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Note>
  All new research should use Builder mode. Classic studies continue to function and collect data normally, but they will not receive new feature updates.
</Note>

## Parts

A **Part** is a single block of the participant experience within a study. A study must have at least one part, and most single-experiment studies have exactly one. You can chain multiple parts to create multi-phase designs — for example, a pre-exposure attitudes measure, a news feed task, and a post-exposure recall phase.

Each part has the following configurable settings:

| Setting | Options | What it controls |
|---|---|---|
| **Post layout** | Feed / Pager | **Feed** shows all posts in a scrolling timeline. **Pager** shows one post at a time and requires an explicit next action to advance. |
| **Interaction requirements** | None / Must react to all / Must react to at least N | Whether participants are required to react to posts before they can proceed to the next part. |
| **Maximum time** | Off / N minutes | If set, the part automatically advances after the time limit expires, even if the participant has not interacted with all posts. |
| **Show reaction buttons** | On / Off | Whether like, dislike, share, and flag buttons are visible beneath each post. |
| **Allow navigating back** | On / Off | In Pager layout, whether participants can return to a previously seen post. |
| **Transition screen** | Optional text | A screen shown between this part and the next, used to give instructions or signal a phase change. |

## Posts

A **Post** is a simulated social-media item that appears inside a part's feed or pager. Posts are created at the study level and then assigned to parts.

### Content fields

Each post carries the following content fields:

- **Source name** — the display name of the outlet or account (e.g., *Breaking News Daily*)
- **Handle** — the @username shown beneath the source name (e.g., *@breakingnewsdaily*)
- **Time ago** — a relative timestamp string displayed on the post (e.g., *"3 hours ago"*)
- **Headline** — the bold title line of the post
- **Content** — the body text of the post
- **Image** — an optional image (JPEG or PNG, max 5 MB) shown inside the post card
- **Engagement counters** — like count, share count, and comment count displayed on the post

### Ground-truth label (`is_true`)

Every post has an `is_true` field — a boolean flag you set when authoring the post. This is the **ground-truth accuracy label**: you mark posts as true or false based on external fact-checking, and Misinfo Research uses this label to compute participant accuracy scores (how often a participant's credibility rating agreed with the objective truth).

The `is_true` label is **never displayed to participants** in any form. It is purely an administrative record used in analyses and exports.

<Warning>
  Do not change the `is_true` label on any post after real participant data has been collected. The label is used to derive accuracy scores for sessions that have already been recorded. Retroactively flipping a label changes the meaning of those scores and can corrupt your dataset.
</Warning>

### A/B content variants

Each post can carry two versions of its content for between-subjects headline or copy manipulations:

- `headline_a` / `headline_b` — two alternative headline strings
- `content_a` / `content_b` — two alternative body text strings

Participants assigned to the **A style condition** see the `_a` variants; those assigned to the **B style condition** see the `_b` variants. If you do not need A/B content manipulation, simply fill in only the `_a` fields and leave the `_b` fields blank (both conditions will then see the same content).

## Conditions

Misinfo Research implements a **2 × 2 between-subjects factorial design** using two independent condition dimensions.

### Style condition (A/B manipulation)

The `style_condition` field on a session records which content variant the participant saw. It takes the value **A** or **B** and determines which headline and body text variant is rendered for every post in the study. This is your primary manipulation for testing whether different framings, headlines, or copy styles affect credibility judgments.

### Metric condition (social proof)

The `metric_condition` field controls the engagement counters displayed beneath each post. You configure a **HIGH** and a **LOW** variant of the counter values (for example, *10,000 likes* vs. *12 likes*). This lets you test whether visible social proof — the apparent popularity of a post — influences how participants rate its accuracy or their willingness to share it.

### Full condition and the 2 × 2 design

Combining the two condition dimensions produces four experimental arms:

| | **Metric: HIGH** | **Metric: LOW** |
|---|---|---|
| **Style: A** | A-HIGH | A-LOW |
| **Style: B** | B-HIGH | B-LOW |

The `full_condition` field on each session stores the combined label (e.g., `A-HIGH`). This is the field you will most commonly use when grouping sessions for analysis.

### Random assignment

In **Builder-mode** studies, each incoming session is assigned to one of the four conditions independently and uniformly at random. Over a large enough sample the cells will be approximately equal in size, but there is no guarantee of exact balance at any given moment during recruitment.

In **classic** studies, assignment uses **permuted block randomization**, which enforces approximate balance in every block of four consecutive sessions, so cell sizes stay close to equal throughout data collection.

## Session

A **Session** represents one participant's complete run through a study — from opening the participant link to reaching the completion screen (or dropping out).

### When a session is created

A session record is created the moment a participant's browser opens the participant URL — **before** the participant reads the consent text, before they click anything. This means your session count on the Dashboard includes participants who opened the link and immediately closed it. Completion rate is calculated against this "sessions started" denominator.

### Condition assignment

The session's condition is assigned at creation time, in the same instant the session record is written to the database. Assignment happens before the participant sees any content, and it does not change.

### Fields stored on a session

| Field | What it records |
|---|---|
| `style_condition` | A or B content variant arm |
| `metric_condition` | HIGH or LOW engagement-counter arm |
| `full_condition` | Combined label, e.g. `A-HIGH` |
| `consented` | Whether the participant clicked the consent button |
| `is_preview` | `1` if the session was started from a preview link; `0` for real participants |
| `demographics` | JSON object with all demographic questionnaire responses |
| `external_id` | Optional identifier passed in the URL by a panel or recruitment system |

### What gets recorded within a session

During the session, Misinfo Research records granular interaction data:

- **Reactions** — each like, dislike, share, or flag action on a post, with a timestamp
- **Post views and dwell time** — when each post entered the viewport and how long it remained visible (used for optional eye-tracking and attention analyses)
- **Post questions** — responses to any post-level questions configured in the Configurator (Builder mode only)
- **Ratings** — credibility or sharing-intention slider values (classic studies)
- **Demographics** — responses to the demographics questionnaire, stored as a JSON object on the session record

## Preview vs production sessions

When you use the **Preview** button in the admin panel, Misinfo Research opens the study URL with `?preview=1` appended. Sessions started this way behave differently from real participant sessions in three important ways.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Fixed Condition" icon="lock" href="/guide/preview-and-launch">
    Preview sessions are always assigned to the **first condition arm** (A-HIGH), regardless of the randomization settings. This makes your preview experience deterministic — you see the same content every time you preview, which makes testing layouts and logic easier.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Logic Skipped" icon="forward" href="/guide/preview-and-launch">
    Conditional logic rules configured in the Configurator **do not fire** during preview sessions. You will see every part of the study in sequence, which lets you inspect all screens without accidentally triggering a skip or redirect branch.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Hidden from Results" icon="eye-slash" href="/guide/preview-and-launch">
    Preview sessions are tagged `is_preview = 1` and are **excluded from the Dashboard, in-app analyses, and all data exports** by default. Running as many preview sessions as you need will not contaminate your real participant data.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Tip>
  To experience the study exactly as a participant would — with real condition assignment, live logic rules, and a session that counts toward your data — open the participant URL in a private/incognito browser window where you are not logged in to the admin panel.
</Tip>

## Completion and drop-out

A session is **started** the moment the participant link is opened. A session is **completed** when the participant reaches the study's final completion screen.

Any session that was started but never completed is counted as a **drop-out**. The Dashboard shows drop-out rate alongside completion rate, and you can inspect the drop-out breakdown by condition to check for differential attrition — a common validity concern in between-subjects designs.

<Note>
  The **raw data export** (both Excel and CSV formats) includes **only completed sessions** by default. Drop-outs are visible on the Dashboard and in the session list, but their partial reaction data is not included in standard exports. If you need to analyse partial data or drop-out patterns, contact your platform administrator for access to the full session log.
</Note>
