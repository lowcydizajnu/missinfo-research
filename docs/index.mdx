Misinfo Research is an open-source, self-hosted platform designed for psychologists and social scientists who study how people engage with online misinformation. You build realistic simulated social-media feeds, assign participants to between-subjects experimental conditions, collect reactions and demographic data, and export everything as analysis-ready Excel or CSV files — all without writing a single line of code. Because the platform runs on your own server, your participants' data never leaves your infrastructure.

## How a study works end-to-end

From setting up a study to downloading your data, the typical research workflow follows these five stages. Each stage is covered in depth in its own guide, but the overview below helps you see how everything connects before you dive in.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a study">
    Log in to the admin panel at `/admin` and click **+ New**. Misinfo Research creates a blank study immediately and drops you on the **Settings** tab. Give the study an internal name, set a short URL slug, and choose your participant-facing title.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add posts">
    Navigate to the **Posts** tab and add the simulated social-media items your participants will see. Each post carries a headline, body text, an optional image, engagement counters (likes, shares, comments), and a ground-truth label that only you can see. You can also supply A/B content variants for between-subjects headline or copy manipulations.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set conditions">
    Open the **Configurator** tab to define how the study flow works — consent text, instructions, demographics placement, and one or more experiment *parts*. Each part can show posts in a scrolling feed or a pager layout, enforce reaction requirements, and impose a time limit. Misinfo Research automatically assigns each incoming session to one of up to four conditions (A/B style × social-proof metric level), giving you a clean 2 × 2 factorial design.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Share the participant link">
    Click **Activate** to make the study live, then copy the participant URL from the **Dashboard** tab. The link takes the form `https://your-host/study/<slug>`. Distribute it via your panel, email list, or recruitment system. Each participant who opens the link starts a new session and is assigned to a condition automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Export your data">
    Once data collection is complete, open the **Export** tab and download an Excel workbook. The file contains separate sheets for raw reactions, session summaries, and a 2 × 2 design breakdown — ready to open in R, Python, SPSS, or Excel.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Key capabilities

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Study Builder">
    The **Builder** mode lets you construct every aspect of your study through a guided **Configurator** tab without touching configuration files. You control consent text, instruction screens, the position of the demographics questionnaire (after consent, at the end, or skipped entirely), and each experiment part. Studies are fully editable after creation and support conditional logic rules, post questions, and multi-part sequences.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="A/B Manipulation">
    Every post can carry two content variants — **Variant A** and **Variant B** — covering the headline, body text, or both. The `style_condition` field on each session records which arm a participant was assigned to, so you can compare credibility ratings, sharing intentions, or any other outcome measure between the two content versions. Assignment is random and balanced across incoming sessions.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Social-Proof Conditions">
    Alongside A/B content manipulation, Misinfo Research supports a **metric condition** that controls the engagement counters displayed beneath each post. You configure a *HIGH* and a *LOW* (or neutral) variant of the counters, letting you test whether visible social proof — likes, shares, comment counts — influences how participants judge a post's accuracy. Combined with the A/B style condition, this gives you a full 2 × 2 between-subjects factorial design out of the box.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Conditional Logic">
    Builder-mode studies support logic rules that change what participants see based on their responses. You can skip parts, show custom transition screens, or redirect participants to different post sets depending on demographic answers or prior reactions. Logic rules are evaluated in production sessions only; they are intentionally bypassed in preview mode so you can test your study flow without triggering unexpected branches.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Dashboard & In-App Analyses">
    The **Dashboard** tab shows a live widget grid with session counts, completion rates, condition breakdowns, and response distributions. Widgets auto-refresh and support date-range filters. For quick exploratory analysis you can run **t-tests**, **one-way ANOVA**, **chi-squared tests**, **Pearson/Spearman correlations**, **linear regression**, and **Cronbach's alpha** directly inside the admin panel — no external software required for an initial look at your data.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Data Export">
    The **Export** tab produces an Excel workbook (.xlsx) or a flat CSV file. The Excel workbook contains multiple sheets: a *Reactions* sheet with one row per post-participant interaction, a *Sessions* sheet with one row per completed session, and a *Design* sheet with the full 2 × 2 condition breakdown. All exports exclude preview sessions by default. You can also retrieve data programmatically through the REST API.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Multi-user Accounts">
    Misinfo Research supports two account roles. **Admin** accounts have full access to every study on the installation, all session data, and user management. **Researcher** accounts can create and manage their own studies and view data only for those studies. This makes it straightforward to run a shared lab installation where multiple team members work on separate projects without interfering with each other's data.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Note>
  The admin panel opens in **Polish** by default. To switch to English, click the language selector in the top-right corner of any admin page. Your preference is saved for the current browser session.
</Note>
