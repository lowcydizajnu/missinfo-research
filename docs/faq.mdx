Misinfo Research is a self-hosted platform, so many questions that would apply to a commercial SaaS product — subscription tiers, data sharing, participant fees — simply don't apply here. The sections below cover the most common questions researchers ask when setting up their first study or scaling to a larger programme.

## Licensing & cost

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Is Misinfo Research free to use?">
    Yes. Misinfo Research is released under the **MIT License** and is completely free to use, modify, and redistribute. There is no hosted service, no subscription tier, and no per-participant fee. You pay only for the infrastructure you choose to run it on — a personal server, a cloud VM, Railway, or anywhere else that can run Node.js.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What does the MIT license allow me to do?">
    The MIT License is one of the most permissive open-source licenses available. You are free to use Misinfo Research for academic research, commercial research, or internal tooling; to modify the source code to suit your needs; and to redistribute modified or unmodified copies. The only requirement is that you retain the original copyright notice. There are no patent clauses, no copyleft obligations, and no requirement to publish your modifications.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Do I need an Anthropic API key to run the platform?">
    No. An Anthropic API key is entirely optional. The platform is fully functional without one — you can build studies, assign participants to conditions, collect responses, and export data with no API key configured at all.

    The key unlocks a single feature: **AI-assisted translation**, which lets you automatically translate post content and interface strings into other languages from within the study builder. If you don't need that feature, you can ignore the `ANTHROPIC_API_KEY` environment variable entirely.

    <Tip>
      If you add an API key later, the translation button will appear automatically in the builder — no restart required beyond setting the environment variable.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Data & privacy

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Where is my study data stored?">
    All data is stored exclusively on your own instance in a **SQLite database file** that lives on the server you control. Nothing is transmitted to the Misinfo Research project, to any third-party analytics service, or to any cloud backend. When you export data, the resulting Excel or CSV file is generated server-side and downloaded directly to your machine.

    <Note>
      If you are running Misinfo Research in Docker or on a platform like Railway, make sure your SQLite file (and the `./uploads/` folder) are stored on a persistent volume so that data survives container restarts.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Are participants anonymous?">
    Yes, by default. Participants do not need to create an account or provide any credentials. Each participant session is identified by a randomly generated **UUID token** that is assigned when they first open the study link. That token has no connection to any real-world identity.

    No IP addresses, browser fingerprints, or device identifiers are stored by the platform itself.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What personally identifiable information (PII) does the platform collect?">
    Out of the box, Misinfo Research collects **no PII at all**. The only information recorded per session is the random UUID token, condition assignment, response data, and timestamps.

    You can introduce PII yourself by adding questions to the demographic form — for example, asking for a participant's age bracket, country, or (if your IRB protocol requires it) an email address for debriefing. If you do this, you are responsible for handling that data in compliance with applicable regulations (GDPR, IRB requirements, etc.), since it lives on your own server.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Does enabling MS Clarity expose participant data?">
    MS Clarity is an optional analytics integration you can enable for behavioural heatmaps and session replays on your study pages. When enabled, Misinfo Research automatically **masks the demographic form** so that any text participants type into those fields is not captured by Clarity. Post content, however, is **not masked by default**, so Clarity may record which posts participants saw.

    If your IRB protocol or data agreement prohibits this, do not enable MS Clarity, or consult Microsoft's documentation on additional masking selectors before activating it.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Study design

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How many conditions can a study have?">
    Misinfo Research uses a two-axis condition model:

    - **Style arms:** Exactly **2** (an A arm and a B arm). You cannot create three or more style conditions. This is an intentional constraint that maps to the standard A/B misinformation-intervention design.
    - **Metric conditions:** Up to **N** independent metric conditions, which you define in the study builder. These are crossed with the style arms to produce your full condition matrix.

    If your research design requires more than two style arms, you would need to run separate studies and recruit participants into each independently.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I run multiple studies at the same time?">
    Yes. Each researcher account can have multiple studies active simultaneously. Studies are fully independent — they have separate slugs, separate condition assignments, and separate data exports. Running studies in parallel does not affect randomisation or data collection in any of them.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What is the difference between classic mode and builder mode?">
    **Builder mode** is the interface you get when you create a study by clicking **+ New** in the admin panel. Builder studies offer a visual drag-and-drop experience for assembling post feeds, and they use **uniform random assignment** to allocate participants to conditions.

    **Classic mode** applies to studies that use a **Settings tab** instead of the builder UI. Classic studies have a separate **credibility rating phase** and use **permuted block randomisation**, which guarantees more even cell sizes but requires a different study flow.

    <Note>
      If you inherited a classic study from a colleague or are re-running a previously published protocol, check which mode it uses before activating it — the two modes behave differently enough to affect your pre-registration.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I edit a study after data collection has started?">
    Yes, you can edit a live study — the admin panel does not lock fields once a study is active. However, some edits will **change the meaning of previously collected data**, making earlier responses incomparable with later ones:

    - Changing the **true/false flag** on a post after participants have rated it.
    - Re-numbering **Likert scale** endpoints (e.g., changing a 1–7 scale to 1–5).
    - Adding, removing, or reordering **conditions**.

    Other edits — fixing a typo in a post caption, adjusting the study title, or adding a new post that will only appear to future participants — are generally safe.

    <Info>
      Design your study fully before activating it. If you must make a breaking change mid-collection, note the change timestamp in your data log and consider treating pre- and post-change data as separate cohorts.
    </Info>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What is the post library, and how does copying work?">
    The **post library** is a shared pool of template posts that all researchers on the instance can browse and reuse. When you copy a post from the library into your study, Misinfo Research creates a **fully independent copy** — changes you make to the copy do not affect the original library entry, and updates to the library entry do not propagate to your study. This means you can safely customise images, captions, and true/false flags without worrying about affecting anyone else's studies.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Common workflow

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do preview sessions work, and how do I clean them up?">
    When you open your own study link while logged in to the admin panel, you have the option to open it as a **preview session**. Preview sessions are excluded from the dashboard statistics and from all data exports by default, so you can test your study flow without polluting your results.

    To review or delete preview sessions, go to the **Export tab** for your study. You will see preview sessions listed separately, and you can delete individual sessions or all previews at once from there.

    <Tip>
      Before sharing your live link with participants, open it yourself as a preview and complete the full flow to check that condition assignment, post order, and the thank-you screen all work as expected.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Where does drop-out data go? Can I see it?">
    Participants who start but do not complete your study are recorded as **partial sessions**. These sessions are **not included in the data export**, because incomplete response sets can distort analysis. However, you can see drop-out information in your study's **dashboard**, which shows a completion rate and a breakdown of how many sessions reached each stage of the study flow. This lets you identify where in the study participants are dropping off without those incomplete records contaminating your export file.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What browsers do participants need? Are there any hardware requirements?">
    Misinfo Research works in any modern browser — Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge are all supported. No plugins or extensions are required.

    The one exception is **eye tracking**, which requires:
    - A **webcam** accessible to the browser.
    - **Stable, consistent lighting** on the participant's face.

    If your study includes an eye-tracking component, communicate these requirements clearly in your recruitment materials, as participants on mobile devices or without a webcam will not be able to complete it.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
