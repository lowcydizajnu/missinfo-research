---

This guide walks you through the complete lifecycle of a Misinfo Research study — from signing in for the first time to downloading your data. Each step is brief by design; linked guides go deeper on every topic. By the end you will have a live study URL you can share with participants and a clear picture of where to find your results.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign in to the admin panel">
    Open your installation's admin panel at `/admin` (for example, `https://your-host/admin`). Enter your username and password, then click **Zaloguj się** (or **Sign in** if the panel is already in English).

    <Note>
      The admin panel opens in **Polish** by default. To switch to English, click the language selector in the **top-right corner** of the page — it shows a flag or language code. Your browser stores the preference as a cookie, so subsequent visits will stay in the language you chose.
    </Note>

    Once you are signed in, your own browser is also marked as an **admin session**. Any study link you open from this browser will be tagged as a preview session (`is_preview = 1`) and excluded from your real participant data automatically.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a new study">
    From the admin home screen, click the **+ New** button (top-right of the studies list). Misinfo Research creates a blank study immediately — you do not need to fill in a form first.

    The new study starts with the placeholder name **"Nowe badanie"** (Polish for "New study") and has a status of **Inactive**, meaning participants cannot access it yet. You land directly on the **Settings** tab of the study editor, ready for the next step.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Name the study and set its slug">
    On the **Settings** tab, fill in two distinct name fields:

    - **Internal name** — used only inside the admin panel to identify the study in lists and exports. Pick something descriptive for your team, such as *"Headline credibility — Wave 2"*.
    - **Participant title** — shown to participants at the top of the study pages. Keep it neutral so it does not prime responses (for example, *"Online News Study"*).

    Then set the **slug** — the short identifier that appears in the participant URL:

    ```
    https://your-host/study/<slug>
    ```

    Choose a slug that is short, lowercase, and contains only letters, numbers, and hyphens (for example, `news-study-w2`).

    <Warning>
      **Do not change the slug after you have shared the participant link.** Changing the slug invalidates all previously distributed URLs, and anyone who opens an old link will see a "study not found" error. Set the slug before you go live and leave it unchanged for the entire data-collection period.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Shape the study flow in the Configurator">
    Click the **Configurator** tab. This is where you define everything participants experience from start to finish.

    Work through the sections from top to bottom:

    1. **Consent text** — paste your IRB-approved consent statement. Participants must click an accept button before proceeding.
    2. **Instruction screen** — toggle this on if you want to show participants a set of instructions after consent and before the feed. You can write custom instruction text.
    3. **Demographics position** — choose when the demographics questionnaire appears:
       - *After consent* — participants fill in age, gender, and other items before seeing any posts.
       - *At the end* — demographics appear after all experiment parts are complete.
       - *Skip* — omit demographics entirely (useful for very short pilots).
    4. **Add an experiment part** — click **+ Add part** to insert a feed block. Give the part a name, choose a layout (**Feed** for a scrolling timeline or **Pager** for one-post-at-a-time navigation), and configure any time limits or reaction requirements. A typical single-part study has one part containing all your posts.

    <Tip>
      You can add multiple parts to create multi-phase studies — for example, a pre-exposure opinion measure, a news feed, and a post-exposure recall task — each with its own layout and settings.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add posts to your study">
    Open the **Posts** tab and click **+ Add post** to create your first simulated social-media item.

    Fill in the post fields:

    | Field | What it controls |
    |---|---|
    | **Source name** | The media outlet or account display name shown above the post |
    | **Handle** | The @username shown beneath the source name |
    | **Time ago** | The relative timestamp displayed on the post (e.g., *"2 hours ago"*) |
    | **Headline** | Bold title text; supply Headline A and Headline B for A/B variants |
    | **Content** | Body text of the post |
    | **True post** | Toggle on if this post describes a real, accurate event (ground-truth label — never shown to participants) |
    | **Engagement counters** | Likes, shares, and comment counts displayed on the post |
    | **Image** | Optional post image; maximum file size is **5 MB**; JPEG and PNG accepted |

    Click **Save post** when each post is ready. Repeat until all posts for the study are added.

    <Note>
      The **True post** toggle stores a ground-truth label (`is_true`) that Misinfo Research uses in analyses comparing participant accuracy ratings to objective truth. It is never displayed to participants in any form.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Preview the study before going live">
    Click the **Preview** button (available on the Settings or Configurator tab). Misinfo Research opens `https://your-host/study/<slug>?preview=1` in a new tab.

    Preview mode has several important properties:

    - The study does **not** need to be active. You can preview an inactive study at any time.
    - The session is always assigned to the **first condition** regardless of randomization settings, so your preview experience is deterministic.
    - **Conditional logic rules do not fire** in preview mode, so you will see every part of the study in sequence.
    - The session is tagged `is_preview = 1` and is excluded from all dashboards, analyses, and exports by default.

    Use preview sessions liberally during design. Run through the full flow — including consent, instructions, the feed, demographics, and the completion screen — to catch any issues before real participants see the study.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Activate the study">
    When you are satisfied with the study design, return to the **Settings** tab and click **Activate**. The study status changes to **Active**, and the participant link is now live.

    <Warning>
      After you begin collecting real participant data, **avoid changing the `is_true` (True post) toggle** on any post. The ground-truth label is used to compute accuracy scores for sessions that have already been collected. Flipping the label retroactively changes the meaning of those scores and can invalidate your dataset.
    </Warning>

    You can deactivate a study at any time to pause data collection without deleting any data.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Share the participant link">
    Go to the **Dashboard** tab of your study. The participant URL is displayed prominently:

    ```
    https://your-host/study/<slug>
    ```

    Copy and distribute this link through your panel system, email list, or any recruitment platform. Each participant who opens the link starts a fresh session and is assigned to a condition automatically.

    <Warning>
      If you open this link from your **own admin browser**, the session will be tagged as a preview session and excluded from data. Use a different browser or a private/incognito window to experience the study exactly as a participant would. The **My sessions** switch on the Dashboard filters the session list to show only your own sessions — turn it off to see real participant data.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Monitor the Dashboard">
    The **Dashboard** tab shows a live overview of your study while data collection is in progress:

    - **Session counts** — total started, completed, and dropped-out
    - **Condition breakdown** — how many sessions have been assigned to each arm of the design
    - **Response distributions** — histograms and frequency tables for reactions and ratings
    - **Completion rate** — percentage of started sessions that reached the end

    Widgets **auto-refresh** periodically. Use the **date-range filter** at the top of the Dashboard to focus on a specific recruitment window. You can also run quick in-app statistical tests (t-test, ANOVA, chi-squared, correlation, regression, Cronbach's alpha) from the **Analyses** section without leaving the platform.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Export your data">
    When data collection is complete, open the **Export** tab.

    Choose your preferred format:

    - **Download Excel (.xlsx)** — a multi-sheet workbook with separate tabs for raw reactions, session summaries, and the 2 × 2 condition design breakdown.
    - **Download CSV** — a flat file with one row per reaction event, suitable for direct import into R, Python, or SPSS.

    Both formats exclude preview sessions. The Excel workbook is the recommended format for most researchers because the separate sheets make it easy to inspect different levels of the data before loading it into your analysis software.
  </Step>
</Steps>
